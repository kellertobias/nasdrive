[`crates/nasfiles-server/src/api/s3/`](crates/nasfiles-server/src/api/s3/mod.rs)
speaks enough of the S3 protocol for ordinary clients (`aws s3`, `rclone`,
backup tools) to read and write NASDrive roots as if they were buckets.

- [`auth.rs`](crates/nasfiles-server/src/api/s3/auth.rs) — the `S3Auth`
  extractor: header SigV4, presigned-URL SigV4, and credential lookup.
- [`mod.rs`](crates/nasfiles-server/src/api/s3/mod.rs) — the small route table
  and the query-parameter dispatch that S3 semantics require, plus
  `resolve_bucket_path`.
- [`object.rs`](crates/nasfiles-server/src/api/s3/object.rs) — ListObjectsV2,
  GetObject, PutObject.
- [`multipart.rs`](crates/nasfiles-server/src/api/s3/multipart.rs) — staged
  multipart uploads and their garbage collection.
- [`etag.rs`](crates/nasfiles-server/src/api/s3/etag.rs) /
  [`xml.rs`](crates/nasfiles-server/src/api/s3/xml.rs) — ETag computation and
  hand-rolled XML serialization.

Mounted at `/s3`, deliberately **outside** the session and CSRF middleware —
every request authenticates itself from scratch.
